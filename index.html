I am a Georgia Tech PhD machine learning researcher with extensive expertise in data science, applied AI, visualization, and explainability. I bring years of leadership experience managing complex technical teams delivering AI solutions. I combine a rigorous research methodology with practical engineering implementations to deliver high-quality, efficient, and cost-effective AI systems that solve customer problems. Take a look at my current CV.

I completed my undergraduate degree in 2011 at Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology in computer science and mathematics. I'm an avid biker.

Summary & Interests

My research blends methods, techniques, and principles from visual analytics and data mining to create new, interactive, scalable tools that empower analysts when exploring, querying, and analyzing large million-edge networks. My work enables analysts to: quickly explore massive graphs from a local perspective (FACETS), visually formulate expressive graph queries with graph autocomplete (VISAGE), and summarize large numbers of graph query results (VIGOR).
